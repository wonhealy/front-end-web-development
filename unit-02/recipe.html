How to Make Cabbage Kimchi

Kimchi is a traditional Korean dish made from fermented vegetables, primarily cabbage. It is packed with flavor and is a staple in Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium napa cabbage
  • 1/4 cup sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Korean chili powder (gochugaru)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 small carrot, julienned

Preparation Steps

  1. Cut the napa cabbage into quarters and sprinkle sea salt between the layers. Let it sit for 2 hours.
  2. Rinse the cabbage thoroughly with water and drain well.
  3. In a large bowl, mix garlic, ginger, fish sauce, sugar, and Korean chili powder to form a paste.
  4. Add the chopped green onions and julienned carrot to the paste and mix well.
  5. Coat each cabbage leaf with the paste mixture, ensuring it is evenly spread.
  6. Pack the cabbage tightly into a clean jar or airtight container.
  7. Leave the jar at room temperature for 1-2 days to ferment. Then store it in the refrigerator.
  8. Enjoy your homemade Kimchi as a side dish or ingredient in your favorite meals!
